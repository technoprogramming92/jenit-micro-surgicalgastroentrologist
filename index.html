What is Surgical Gastroenterologist?

A gastroenterologist is a doctor who specializes in diagnosing and treating conditions related to the digestive system. This includes the stomach, intestines, liver, pancreas, and other organs involved in digestion. They help manage issues like acid reflux, ulcers, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), Crohn's disease, and liver diseases. Gastroenterologists use various tests and procedures, such as endoscopies and colonoscopies, to examine and treat these conditions, aiming to improve patients' digestive health and overall well-being.

What conditions is cured by Gastroenterologist?

Gastroenterology is commonly used for a wide range of treatments, including:

  • Colorectal Surgery: Removes tumors or diseased sections of the colon and rectum, often for conditions like colorectal cancer, polyps, or diverticulitis.
  • Laparoscopic Surgery: Minimally invasive procedures using small incisions, often for conditions such as hernias, gallstones, and appendicitis.
  • Bariatric Surgery: Aids in weight loss for patients with obesity, reducing the risk of related health conditions like diabetes and heart disease.
  • HPB Surgery: Treats diseases of the liver, pancreas, and biliary system, including tumors, cysts, and chronic pancreatitis.
